Kelewele

Kelewele (Spicy Fried Plantains) is a popular street food in Ghana, especially in cities like Accra. It is made from ripe plantains cut into small pieces, seasoned with a mix of spices, and fried until golden brown and slightly crispy.
Kelewele is known for its sweet and spicy flavor. The natural sweetness of ripe plantains combines perfectly with spices like ginger, pepper, and sometimes garlic, creating a rich and bold taste. The outside becomes slightly crispy while the inside remains soft and tender.
This snack is usually sold by street vendors in the evenings, where the aroma of frying plantains fills the air. It is often served hot and sometimes paired with roasted groundnuts, which add a crunchy texture and make the dish even more enjoyable.
Kelewele is loved because it is quick to prepare, affordable, and very satisfying. It can be eaten as a snack, side dish, or even a light meal. Many people enjoy it on the go, making it one of the most convenient foods in Ghana.
The preparation is simple, but the secret lies in the seasoning. The right mix of spices gives kelewele its signature taste and makes it stand out from regular fried plantains.

Kelewele is more than just a snack—it is part of Ghana’s street food culture. It brings people together, especially in the evenings, and is enjoyed by both locals and visitors.
Whether bought from a roadside vendor or made at home, Kelewele remains a flavorful, spicy, and irresistible treat in Ghanaian cuisine.

Ingredients 🥕

  1. Ripe plantains 🍌
  2. Ginger
  3. Pepper 🌶️
  4. Garlic (optional)
  5. Salt
  6. Vegetable oil (for frying)

Steps to Make Kelewele👩‍🍳

1. Peel and Cut Plantains

  • Peel ripe plantains
  • Cut into cubes or slices

2. Prepare Spice Mix

  • Blend or crush ginger, pepper, and garlic
  • Add a little salt

3. Season the Plantains

  • Mix plantains with the spice mixture
  • Ensure all pieces are well coated

4. Heat Oil

  • Pour oil into a pan
  • Heat until ready for frying

5. Fry the Plantains ⏱ (10–15 minutes)

  • Add plantains to hot oil
  • Fry until golden brown
  • Turn occasionally

6. Drain Oil

  • Remove and place on paper to drain excess oil

7. Serve

  • Serve hot
  • Add groundnuts if desired
  • Enjoy

Tipes 💡

  • Use ripe but firm plantains for best results 🍌
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan while frying
  • Adjust pepper to your spice level 🌶️
  • Serve hot for best taste 🔥
