Konkonte & Palmnut Soup

Konkonte & Palmnut Soup (popularly called “Face the Wall & Palmnut Soup”) is a rich and traditional Ghanaian meal enjoyed across Ghana. The nickname “Face the Wall” comes from how seriously people focus on eating it—because of its delicious and satisfying nature.
Konkonte is made from dried cassava that has been ground into flour and cooked into a smooth, stretchy dough. It has a slightly earthy taste and a firm texture that makes it perfect for dipping into rich soups. Unlike fufu, konkonte is usually darker in color and has a unique traditional flavor.
Palmnut soup, known locally as abenkwan, is a thick and flavorful soup made from palm fruit extract. It is cooked with tomatoes, onions, pepper, and a variety of proteins such as meat, fish, or crab. The soup has a rich, oily texture and a deep, satisfying taste that blends perfectly with konkonte.
This dish is very popular in many Ghanaian homes and is often prepared for family meals and special occasions. It is known for being very filling and nutritious, providing both energy and essential nutrients.
Eating Konkonte & Palmnut Soup is also a cultural experience. Traditionally, the konkonte is eaten with the hands by dipping it into the soup and swallowing, allowing the rich flavors to stand out.

Despite its simple ingredients, this dish delivers a powerful taste. It shows how traditional Ghanaian cooking transforms basic foods into something special and satisfying.
Whether enjoyed at home or at a local food spot, Konkonte & Palmnut Soup remains a symbol of tradition, flavor, and comfort in Ghanaian cuisine.

Ingredients 🥕

For Konkonte:

  1. Cassava flour
  2. Water

For Palmnut Soup:

  1. Palm fruits or palmnut paste
  2. Tomatoes
  3. Onion
  4. Pepper
  5. Meat, fish, or crab
  6. Salt
  7. Seasoning cubes

Steps to Make Konkonte & Palmnut Soup👩‍🍳

How to prepare Palmnut Soup:

1. Extract Palmnut Juice

  • Boil palm fruits
  • Pound and extract juice (or use ready paste)

2. Cook the Meat

  • Season meat with salt, onion, and spices
  • Cook until tender

3. Prepare the Base

  • Blend tomatoes, pepper, and onions
  • Add to the meat

4. Add Palmnut Extract

  • Pour palmnut juice into the pot
  • Stir well

5. Cook the Soup

  • Allow soup to cook well
  • Stir occasionally

6. Final Taste

  • Add salt and seasoning
  • Adjust taste

How to prepare Konkonte:

1. Boil Water

  • Heat water until boiling

2. Add Cassava Flour

  • Add flour gradually
  • Stir continuously

3. Cook and Stir

  • Stir until smooth and lump-free
  • Cook until thick

4. Shape the Konkonte

  • Mold into portions

5. Serve

  • Serve hot with palmnut soup
  • Enjoy

Tipes 💡

  • Stir konkonte well to avoid lumps
  • Use fresh palmnut for richer taste 🌴
  • Don’t make the soup too watery
  • Serve hot for best experience 🔥
