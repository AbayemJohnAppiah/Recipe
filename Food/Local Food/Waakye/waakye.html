Waakye

Waakye (Rice & Beans) is a beloved traditional dish enjoyed across Ghana. It is made by cooking rice together with beans, often with dried millet leaves, which give the dish its unique color and rich flavor.
Waakye is more than just rice and beans—it is a complete meal that is usually served with a variety of sides such as fried plantain, boiled eggs, spaghetti, salad, meat, fish, and the famous spicy black pepper sauce known as shito. This combination makes it one of the most satisfying and filling meals you can find.
The beans are cooked until soft, and then rice is added and cooked together, allowing the flavors to blend perfectly. The millet leaves used during cooking give waakye its traditional brownish color and a slightly earthy taste that makes it special.
Waakye is very popular as a breakfast or lunch meal and is commonly sold by street vendors across Ghana. Many people enjoy it because it is affordable, nutritious, and very tasty. It is also flexible-you can choose your own combination of sides depending on your preference.

This dish is deeply connected to Ghanaian culture and daily life. It is often eaten on busy mornings or during work breaks because it is both quick to serve and very filling.
Whether bought from a roadside vendor or prepared at home, Waakye represents the rich flavors, culture, and everyday lifestyle of Ghanaian food.

Ingredients 🥕

For Waakye:

  1. Rice
  2. Beans (black-eyed peas or red beans)
  3. Sorghum leaves (waakye leaves)
  4. Water
  5. Salt

Optional Sides:

  1. Fried plantain 🍌
  2. Boiled eggs 🥚
  3. Spaghetti
  4. Meat or fish 🍖🐟
  5. Shito (black pepper sauce)

Steps to Make Waakye👩‍🍳

1. Wash the Beans

  • Wash beans thoroughly
  • Remove dirt and stones

2. Cook the Beans

  • Boil beans in water
  • Add waakye leaves
  • Cook until beans are soft

3. Add Rice

  • Wash rice
  • Add to the cooked beans

4. Cook Together ⏱ (20–25 minutes)

  • Add water if needed
  • Allow rice and beans to cook together

5. Add Salt

  • Add salt to taste
  • Stir gently

6. Steam the Waakye ⏱ (5–10 minutes)

  • Cover and allow it to steam
  • Ensure it is well cooked

7. Remove Leaves

  • Take out the millet leaves

8. Serve

  • Serve hot
  • Add plantain, egg, meat, or shito

Tipes 💡

  • Use waakye leaves for authentic color 🍃
  • Don’t overcook the rice
  • Add enough water but not too much
  • Serve with shito for best taste 🌶️
