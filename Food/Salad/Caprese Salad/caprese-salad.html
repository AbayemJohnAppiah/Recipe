Caprese Salad

Caprese Salad is a simple yet elegant dish that comes from Italy. It is known for its fresh ingredients, beautiful colors, and light, natural flavor.
This salad is made with just a few key ingredients-fresh tomatoes, soft mozzarella cheese, and basil leaves-but together they create a rich and refreshing taste. The red tomatoes, white cheese, and green basil also represent the colors of the Italian flag, making the dish both meaningful and visually attractive.
Caprese salad is loved for its simplicity. There is no heavy cooking or complicated preparation. Instead, the focus is on using fresh, high-quality ingredients that naturally taste good. The tomatoes provide sweetness and juiciness, the mozzarella adds a soft and creamy texture, and the basil gives a fresh and slightly herbal flavor.
The dressing is usually just olive oil, sometimes with a little salt and pepper. This keeps the salad light while enhancing the natural flavors of the ingredients without overpowering them.
Caprese salad is often served as a starter (appetizer), side dish, or light meal. It is especially popular during warm weather because it is refreshing and easy to eat.

Because of its simple ingredients and beautiful presentation, it is commonly found in restaurants and is also very easy to prepare at home.
Whether served at a casual meal or a special occasion, Caprese salad is a fresh, simple, and elegant dish that shows how basic ingredients can create amazing flavor.

Ingredients 🥕

  1. Tomatoes 🍅
  2. Fresh mozzarella cheese 🧀
  3. Basil leaves 🌿
  4. Olive oil
  5. Salt
  6. Pepper

Steps to Make Caprese Salad👩‍🍳

1. Prepare Ingredients

  • Wash tomatoes and basil
  • Slice tomatoes and mozzarella

2. Arrange on Plate

  • Place tomato and cheese slices alternately
  • Add basil leaves between them

3. Add Dressing

  • Drizzle olive oil
  • Add salt and pepper

4. Serve

  • Serve fresh
  • Enjoy

Tipes 💡

  • Use fresh, ripe tomatoes 🍅
  • Use soft, fresh mozzarella
  • Don’t overuse oil
  • Serve immediately for best taste
