Chin Chin

Chin Chin is a sweet, crunchy, and slightly buttery snack made from flour, sugar, milk, and butter, then cut into small pieces and deep-fried until golden brown. It is widely enjoyed in Ghana, Nigeria, and other parts of West Africa.
Chin chin has a firm and crunchy texture that makes it very satisfying to eat. It is slightly sweet and can be flavored with vanilla, nutmeg, or other spices to give it a richer taste.
One of the best features of chin chin is its long shelf life. When properly fried and stored in an airtight container, it stays crunchy for many days, making it perfect for travel, parties, or everyday snacking.
Chin chin is often made in large batches and shared during celebrations, holidays, and family gatherings. It is a snack that brings people together and is loved by both children and adults.
The appearance is golden yellow to light brown, with small cube or strip shapes. Its simple look hides its rich and addictive taste.
Chin chin can also be made softer or harder depending on how it is prepared. Some versions are very crunchy, while others are slightly softer and easier to bite.

Making chin chin at home is simple and requires only basic ingredients. It involves mixing dough, cutting it into shapes, and frying until crispy.
Whether enjoyed as a daily snack or festive treat, chin chin is a crunchy, sweet, and satisfying snack that delivers simple ingredients, rich flavor, and lasting crunch in every bite.

Ingredients 🥕

  1. Flour 🌾
  2. Sugar 🍯
  3. Butter 🧈
  4. Milk 🥛
  5. Egg 🥚 (optional)
  6. Nutmeg or vanilla 🌿
  7. Oil for frying

Steps to Make Chin Chin👩‍🍳

1. Mix Ingredients

  • Combine flour, sugar, butter, milk, and spices
  • Mix into dough

2. Knead Dough

  • Knead until smooth and firm

3. Roll Dough

  • Flatten dough on surface

4. Cut Shapes

  • Cut into small cubes or strips

5. Heat Oil

  • Heat oil in deep pan

6. Fry Chin Chin

  • Fry until golden brown and crispy

7. Drain Oil

  • Place on paper towel

8. Cool & Serve

  • Let cool and enjoy

Tipes 💡

  • Don’t over-fry or it becomes too hard 🔥
  • Cut evenly for uniform cooking
  • Add nutmeg for better aroma 🌿
  • Store in airtight container for freshness
