Plantain Chips

Plantain Chips are crispy, golden, and flavorful snacks made from thin slices of unripe or semi-ripe plantains that are deep-fried until crunchy. They are widely enjoyed for their simple preparation, natural taste, and satisfying crunch.
Plantain chips have a light, crispy texture and a slightly sweet or salty flavor depending on how they are prepared. When fried, the plantain slices become golden and crunchy, making them very enjoyable to eat as a snack on their own or with dips.
One of the best features of plantain chips is their versatility. They can be made salty, spicy, or even slightly sweet, depending on personal preference. This makes them suitable for different tastes and occasions.
Plantain chips are also a common street snack in many countries, especially in Ghana and other parts of West Africa. They are often sold in small bags and enjoyed as a quick snack at school, work, or during travel.
The appearance of plantain chips is golden yellow to light brown, with thin, crispy slices that are easy to bite. They are usually served in bags, bowls, or containers for sharing.
They are also easy to store and carry, making them a convenient snack for busy days. When properly fried and stored, they stay crunchy for a long time.

Making plantain chips at home is simple and requires only a few ingredients. The key is slicing the plantains evenly and frying them at the right temperature for maximum crispiness.
Whether enjoyed as a quick snack or homemade treat, plantain chips are a crispy, golden, and flavorful snack that delivers crunch, taste, and satisfaction in every bite.

Ingredients 🥕

  1. Unripe or semi-ripe plantains 🍌
  2. Vegetable oil
  3. Salt 🧂
  4. Optional spices (pepper, chili, sugar) 🌶️🍯

Steps to Make Plantain Chips👩‍🍳

1. Peel Plantain

  • Remove plantain skin carefully

2. Slice Thinly

  • Cut into very thin round slices

3. Heat Oil

  • Heat oil in a deep pan

4. Fry Plantains

  • Fry slices until golden and crispy

5. Remove & Drain

  • Place on paper towel to remove oil

6. Season

  • Add salt or spices while still warm

7. Serve ⏱ (Immediately)

  • Let cool and enjoy

Tipes 💡

  • Use unripe plantain for extra crunch 🍌
  • Slice evenly for uniform frying
  • Don’t overcrowd the pan
  • Store in airtight container for freshness
