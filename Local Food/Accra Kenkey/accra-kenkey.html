Accra Kenkey

Accra Kenkey (also called Ga Kenkey) is a traditional staple food widely enjoyed in Ghana, especially among the Ga people in coastal areas like Accra. It is made from fermented maize dough that is partially cooked, wrapped in corn husks, and then boiled until firm and ready to eat.
Kenkey has a slightly sour taste due to the fermentation process, which gives it a unique flavor loved by many. Its firm, dough-like texture makes it perfect for pairing with a variety of accompaniments such as fried fish, pepper sauce, and shito.
The preparation of Ga kenkey is traditional and requires patience. The maize dough is first fermented for a few days, then part of it is cooked to form a smooth paste called “aflata.” This cooked portion is mixed back with the raw dough, shaped, wrapped carefully in corn husks, and boiled. This method gives kenkey its special taste and texture.
Accra Kenkey is commonly eaten for lunch or dinner and is very popular at street food spots, homes, and local restaurants. It is often served with fried fish, fresh pepper sauce, and sometimes sliced onions and tomatoes, making it a complete and satisfying meal.

This dish is more than just food—it is part of Ghanaian culture and identity. It represents tradition, community, and the rich heritage of the Ga people. Eating kenkey, especially with your hands, adds to the authentic experience.
Whether enjoyed at the beach, at home, or from a street vendor, Accra Kenkey remains one of the most iconic and cherished meals in Ghanaian cuisine.

Ingredients 🥕

For Accra Kenkey:

  1. Fermented maize dough
  2. Water
  3. Corn husks (for wrapping)

Optional Sides:

  1. Fried fish 🐟
  2. Pepper sauce 🌶️
  3. Shito
  4. Fresh vegetables 🥗

Steps to Make Accra Kenkey👩‍🍳

1. Prepare the Dough ⏱ (2–3 days for fermentation)

  • Soak and grind maize into dough
  • Allow it to ferment for 2–3 days

2. Cook Part of the Dough (Aflata)

  • Take a portion of the dough
  • Cook it in a pot
  • Stir until it becomes >thick and smooth

3. Mix the Dough

  • Add the cooked dough (aflata) back to the raw dough
  • Mix thoroughly

4. Shape the Kenkey

  • Mold into medium-sized balls

5. Wrap in Corn Husks

  • Wrap each ball tightly with corn husks

6. Boil the Kenkey ⏱ (2 – 3 hours)

  • Place wrapped kenkey in a pot
  • Add water and boil until fully cooked

7. Serve

  • Remove from husks
  • Serve hot with fish and pepper sauce

Tipes 💡

  • Proper fermentation gives the best taste
  • Wrap tightly to keep shape
  • Boil well to ensure it cooks inside
  • Serve hot for best flavor 🔥
