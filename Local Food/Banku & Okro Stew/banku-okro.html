Banku & Okro Stew

Banku & Okro Stew is one of the most loved traditional meals in Ghana. It is a rich and satisfying dish that combines smooth, stretchy banku with a flavorful and slightly slimy okro stew filled with fish, meat, or seafood.
Banku is made from a mixture of fermented corn and cassava dough, cooked and stirred continuously until it becomes smooth, soft, and slightly stretchy. Its mild sour taste makes it a perfect match for the rich and spicy stew.
Okro stew is prepared using fresh okro (okra), palm oil, tomatoes, onions, pepper, and a variety of proteins like fish, crab, or meat. The okro gives the stew its unique texture, which helps it blend perfectly with the banku when eaten. The stew is often rich, slightly spicy, and full of flavor.
This dish is widely enjoyed across coastal and inland communities in Ghana and is commonly served at home, local food joints, and special gatherings. It is especially popular because it is both nutritious and filling.

Eating Banku & Okro Stew is also a cultural experience. Traditionally, it is eaten with the hands, where small portions of banku are dipped into the stew and enjoyed together. The combination of the soft banku and the rich, flavorful stew creates a delicious balance that many people love.
Whether served with fresh fish, meat, or seafood, Banku & Okro Stew remains a true taste of Ghanaian tradition and comfort food.

Ingredients 🥕

For Banku:

  1. Corn dough
  2. Cassava dough
  3. Water
  4. Salt

For Okro Stew:

  1. Fresh okro (okra)
  2. Palm oil
  3. Tomatoes
  4. Onion
  5. Pepper
  6. Fish, meat, or crab
  7. Salt
  8. Seasoning cubes

Steps to Make Banku & Okro Stew 👩‍🍳

How to prepare Banku:

1. Mix the Dough

  • Mix corn dough and cassava dough with water
  • Stir until smooth

2. Cook and Stir ⏱ (20–40 minutes)

  • Put the mixture on fire
  • Stir continuously to avoid lumps
  • Cook until it becomes thick and stretchy

3. Shape the Banku

  • Mold into round balls
  • Set aside

How to prepare Okro Stew:

1. Prepare the Okro

  • Wash and chop the okro into small pieces

2. Cook the Base ⏱ (10–15 minutes)

  • Heat palm oil
  • Add onions, pepper, and tomatoes
  • Fry well

3. Add Protein ⏱ (10–15 minutes)

  • Add fish, meat, or crab
  • Cook until tender

4. Add Okro ⏱ (10 minutes)

  • Add chopped okro
  • Stir and allow it to cook
  • Add salt and seasoning

5. Final Cooking ⏱ (5–10 minutes)

  • Let everything cooktogether
  • Stir gently

Tipes 💡

  • Stir banku well to avoid lumps
  • Use fresh okro for better texture
  • Don’t overcook okro (it loses its freshness)
  • Add seafood for richer taste 🦀🐟
