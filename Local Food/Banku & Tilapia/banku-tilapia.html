Banku & Tilapia

Banku & Tilapia is a classic and highly loved dish in Ghana, especially in coastal areas. It is a delicious combination of soft, slightly sour banku served with well-seasoned grilled tilapia and spicy pepper sauce.
Banku is made from a mixture of fermented corn and cassava dough, cooked and stirred continuously until it becomes smooth, stretchy, and slightly sour in taste. Its soft texture and mild flavor make it the perfect base for enjoying rich and spicy sauces.
The tilapia is usually marinated with a blend of spices, onions, ginger, and pepper, then grilled over charcoal to give it a smoky flavor. The outside becomes slightly crispy while the inside remains soft and juicy, making it very flavorful and enjoyable.
This dish is often served with a fresh and spicy pepper sauce made from blended pepper, onions, tomatoes, and sometimes oil. Some people also add shito (black pepper sauce) or fresh vegetables like cabbage and tomatoes to enhance the taste.
Banku & Tilapia is commonly eaten at local chop bars, beaches, and restaurants, where it is enjoyed in a relaxed and social setting. It is a favorite for many people because of its bold flavors and satisfying combination of textures.

Eating this dish is also a cultural experience. Traditionally, banku is eaten with the hands by pinching a portion, dipping it into the pepper sauce, and combining it with pieces of fish.
Whether enjoyed by the roadside or prepared at home, Banku & Tilapia represents the rich flavors, culture, and lifestyle of Ghanaian cuisine.

Ingredients 🥕

For Banku:

  1. Corn dough
  2. Cassava dough
  3. Water
  4. Salt

For Grilled Tilapia:

  1. Fresh tilapia
  2. Onion
  3. Ginger
  4. Pepper
  5. Salt
  6. Seasoning cubes

For Pepper Sauce:

  1. Fresh pepper
  2. Onion
  3. Tomatoes
  4. Oil
  5. Salt

Steps to Make Banku & Tilapia 👩‍🍳

How to prepare Banku:

1. Mix the Dough

  • Mix corn dough and
    cassava dough with water
  • Stir until smooth

2. Cook and Stir ⏱ (20–40 minutes)

  • Put the mixture on fire
  • Stir continuously to avoid
    lumps
  • Cook until it becomes
    thick and stretchy

3. Shape the Banku

  • Mold into round balls
  • Set aside

How to prepare Grilled Tilapia:

1. Clean and Season Fish

  • Wash the tilapia
  • Blend onion, ginger,
    and pepper
  • Rub the mixture on
    the fish
  • Add salt and seasoning

2. Marinate ⏱ (15–20 minutes)

  • Allow the fish to
    absorb the spices

3. Grill the Tilapia ⏱ (20–30 minutes)

  • Grill over charcoal or
    oven
  • Turn occasionally
  • Cook until golden
    and well done

How to prepare Pepper Sauce:

1. Blend Ingredients

  • Blend pepper, onion,
    and tomatoes

2. Serve

  • Serve hot banku with
    grilled tilapia
  • Add pepper sauce

Tipes 💡

  • Use charcoal grill for
    best smoky taste 🔥
  • Don’t overcook banku
    (avoid too hard texture)
  • Let fish marinate well
    for deeper flavor
  • Serve hot for best
    experience
