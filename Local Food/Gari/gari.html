Gari Soakings

Gari Soakings (Gari & Sugar) is one of the simplest and most loved quick meals in Ghana. It is made by soaking gari (processed cassava granules) in water and adding sugar, milk, and other toppings to create a refreshing and satisfying dish.
Gari is made from cassava that has been grated, fermented, and fried into dry, crunchy granules. When soaked in water, it softens slightly while still keeping a light crunchy texture, making it very enjoyable to eat.
Gari soakings is popular because it is fast, affordable, and easy to prepare. It requires no cooking, which makes it perfect for students, busy workers, and anyone who wants a quick meal. It is often eaten as a snack, breakfast, or even dinner.
What makes this dish special is how customizable it is. People add different ingredients like sugar, milk, groundnuts, coconut, or even bread to enhance the taste. Some prefer it very sweet, while others enjoy a balanced taste.
This dish is also very refreshing, especially in hot weather. When cold water is used, it becomes a cool and enjoyable meal that helps beat the heat.

Gari Soakings is more than just a quick meal-it is part of everyday life in Ghana. It is commonly eaten at home, in schools, and even at workplaces because of how easy and satisfying it is.
Whether kept simple or loaded with toppings, Gari & Sugar remains a classic, affordable, and comforting Ghanaian dish.

Ingredients 🥕

For Gari:

  1. Gari
  2. Water (preferably cold)
  3. Sugar
  4. Milk (optional)

Optional Sides:

  1. Groundnuts 🥜
  2. Coconut 🥥
  3. Bread 🍞
  4. Milo or chocolate powder 🍫

Steps to Make Gari Soakings👩‍🍳

1. Add Gari to Bowl

  • Pour gari into a bowl

2. Add Water

  • Add cold water
  • Stir gently

3. Adjust Water

  • Pour away excess water if needed
  • Leave a little for soaking

4. Add Sugar

  • Add sugar to your taste
  • Mix well

5. Add Milk

  • Pour milk for richer taste

6. Add Toppings

  • Add groundnuts, coconut, or bread
  • Mix or leave on top

7. Serve

  • Serve immediately
  • Enjoy fresh

Tipes 💡

  • Use cold water for best experience ❄️
  • Don’t add too much water (avoid soggy gari)
  • Add plenty of groundnuts for crunch 🥜
  • Eat immediately for best texture
