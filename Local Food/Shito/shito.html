Shito

Shito (Black Pepper Sauce) is one of the most popular and flavorful condiments in Ghana. It is a rich, dark, spicy sauce made from pepper, onions, oil, dried fish, and spices, cooked slowly until it develops a deep flavor and long shelf life.
Shito is known for its bold taste and versatility. It can be eaten with almost any dish-rice, waakye, kenkey, bread, yam, and even snacks. Its spicy and slightly smoky flavor makes it a favorite for people who enjoy strong and rich tastes.
The preparation of shito takes time, but the result is worth it. The ingredients are carefully blended and fried in oil for a long period, allowing the flavors to combine and deepen. The slow cooking process is what gives shito its dark color, thick texture, and intense taste.
One of the special things about shito is that it can be stored for a long time without spoiling, especially when cooked properly. This makes it very convenient to prepare in large quantities and use over time.

Shito is not just a sauce-it is part of everyday Ghanaian life. It is often served as a side dish and can completely change the taste of a meal, making simple food more exciting and enjoyable.
Whether homemade or bought from a local vendor, Shito represents bold flavor, tradition, and the love of spicy food in Ghanaian cuisine.

Ingredients 🥕

  1. Fresh pepper 🌶️
  2. Onion
  3. Garlic & ginger
  4. Tomato paste
  5. Dried fish or shrimp
    (powdered)
  6. Vegetable oil
  7. Salt
  8. Seasoning cubes

Steps to Make Shito👩‍🍳

1. Prepare Ingredients

  • Wash pepper, onions,
    garlic, and ginger
  • Blend or grind into a
    smooth mixture

2. Heat Oil

  • Pour a large amount of
    oil into a pot
  • Heat until hot

3. Fry Onion Base

  • Add chopped onions
  • Fry until golden

4. Add Blended Mixture

  • Pour in the blended
    pepper mixture
  • Stir well

5. Add Tomato Paste

  • Add tomato paste
  • Stir and fry until
    well mixed

6. Add Fish or Shrimp Powder

  • Add powdered dried
    fish or shrimp
  • Mix well

7. Slow Cooking ⏱ (30–45 minutes)

  • Cook on low heat
  • Stir regularly to
    avoid burning
  • Allow oil to rise
    to the top

8. Season

  • Add salt and seasoning
    cubes
  • Taste and adjust

9. Cool & Store

  • Allow shito to cool
  • Store in a clean container

Tipes 💡

  • Cook on low heat for
    best flavor 🔥
  • Stir regularly to
    prevent burning
  • Use enough oil for
    preservation
  • Store in airtight
    container
