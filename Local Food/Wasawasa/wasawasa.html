Wasawasa

Wasawasa is a traditional dish from the northern regions of Ghana, especially among communities in Tamale and surrounding areas. It is made from dried yam that is ground into flour, then steamed to create a soft, light, and slightly crumbly meal.
Wasawasa is known for its simple preparation and unique texture. Unlike many other Ghanaian dishes, it is not boiled or fried but steamed, which makes it light and easy to digest. The yam flour absorbs moisture during steaming, forming small, soft granules that are fluffy and satisfying to eat.
This dish is usually served with a spicy pepper sauce, vegetables, or fried fish. Some people also add oil, onions, or meat to enhance its flavor. The combination of the mild yam taste with spicy and savory toppings creates a delicious and balanced meal.
Wasawasa is not only tasty but also nutritious. Yam is a good source of energy, and because the dish is steamed, it retains many of its natural nutrients. It is a popular meal for both young and old and is often eaten for breakfast or lunch.

In Northern Ghana, Wasawasa is more than just food-it is part of the culture and daily life. It is commonly sold in local markets and by street vendors, making it easily accessible and affordable.
Whether enjoyed at home or bought from a vendor, Wasawasa represents the simplicity, culture, and rich food traditions of Northern Ghana.

Ingredients 🥕

For Wasawasa:

  1. Yam flour
  2. Water
  3. Salt
  4. Oil (optional)

Optional Sides:

  1. Pepper sauce 🌶️
  2. Fried fish 🐟
  3. Vegetables 🥗
  4. Meat 🍖

Steps to Make Wasawasa👩‍🍳

1. Prepare the Flour

  • Put yam flour in a bowl
  • Add a little water gradually
  • Mix to form small lumps (not too wet)

2. Set Up Steaming Pot

  • Add water to a pot
  • Place a steaming basket or sieve on top

3. Steam the Wasawasa ⏱ (20–30 minutes)

  • Put the yam mixture into the steamer
  • Cover and allow it to cook
  • Stir occasionally for even cooking

4. Break and Fluff

  • Remove and break any lumps
  • Fluff to make it soft and light

5. Add Salt & Oil

  • Add salt to taste
  • Add a little oil if desired

6. Final Steam (Optional)

  • Steam again briefly for better texture

7. Serve

  • Serve hot with pepper sauce or fish
  • Enjoy

Tipes 💡

  • Don’t add too much water (it will become sticky)
  • Stir during steaming to avoid lumps
  • Use fresh yam flour for best taste
  • Serve hot for best experience 🔥
